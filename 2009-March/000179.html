I was trying to find out a way to eliminate the last step within Notepad or
similar. 
 
Using the option#2  you can leave empty cells using NA instead "blank":
 
> HI.2 = c(HI, rep(NA,21))
> SI.2 = c(SI, rep(NA,9))
 
and then substitute NA by empty cells in the arguments of function
write.table:

> write.table(ICout2, "ICout2.txt", row.names=F, col.names=F, quote=F,
sep=",", na="")

This substitute all the NAs. I am not sure but I think that INTERCATH asks
for NAs in missing character cells. A possible solution to keep the "real"
NAs is to deal them as character (as "-9"):
 
> HI =
c("HI","SE","2005","Quarter","1","testA","SubDiv","BAL25","NA","NA","-9","NA
")

country")
