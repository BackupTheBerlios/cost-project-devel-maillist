>     $time is a factor yyyy – 1 to yyyy – 4  when timestrata = “quarter”
> 
>     $time is a factor yyyy – 1 to yyyy – 12  when timestrata = “month”
> 
>     and
> 
>     $time is a factor yyyy    when time = “year”
> 
>     and a factor      “all”   when timestrata is unspecified
> 
>      
> 
>     So the problem I am encountering is that there is no way of
>     distinguishing eg 2005 – 1 from 1^st quarter 2005 and Jan 2005
>     unless you pass your function Mathieu’s “strDef” object which
>     defines how the user has stratified the data.
> 
>     To my mind it would make things very much easier if how the user has
>     stratified the data was apparent from the data itself.
> 
>      
> 
>     What does anybody think about $time in a consolidated class having
>     the form yyyy – q – m  for stratification by month so eg 2005 – 1 –
>     1 would be jan 2005 and 2005 – 1 would remain as 1^st quarter 2005.   
> 
>     Or is it too late for such changes? Would that cause more problems
>     than it solves?
